# CYA-1 Reflection

Note: Claude Sonnet was used to enhance my writing.

## Activity Description
This semester, I applied to 13 internships with the goal of securing a new position in cybersecurity or software engineering. The expectation was to actively pursue opportunities beyond my existing return offer, research target companies, tailor application materials, and put myself in a position to land a role that offers more growth than returning to my previous employer.

## Technical or Professional Decisions
The main strategic decision I made was to lean into my interpersonal skills as my primary differentiator. In a field where many candidates have similar technical backgrounds, I felt that my communication style and ability to connect with people was a genuine edge. I also made a deliberate choice to seek out professional feedback when possible — taking advantage of resume review opportunities rather than going it completely alone — which reflected an understanding that outside perspective improves output.

## Contributions
The job search was largely an independent effort. I researched roles, tailored my materials, and managed the application process on my own. When I had access to professionals for resume feedback, I took initiative to use those resources, which showed self-awareness about where collaboration adds value. I received two interviews out of 13 applications, which required the work I put into presenting myself as a strong candidate on paper.

## Quality Assessment
Overall, I think the quality of what I did submit was solid — two interviews from 13 applications is a reasonable conversion rate, and it suggests my materials and pitch were resonating. That said, I know I left opportunities on the table. I could have applied to more positions and been more proactive about following up with recruiters after submitting applications. The search felt more passive than it could have been, and if I were to repeat this, I'd pair the quality I brought to each application with a higher volume and more intentional outreach after applying.

## Career Alignment
This experience moved me closer to my career goals by pushing me to actively pursue something better rather than defaulting to what's comfortable. I have a return offer from last summer, which provides security, but I recognized that real growth requires seeking out new challenges. Targeting cybersecurity roles specifically helped me think more clearly about where I want to specialize long-term, and the two interviews I landed gave me real practice articulating my skills and goals in a professional setting — experience that will compound as I continue searching.
